FAQ

What's all the green and black on my patio?
Green stains on wood are usually the result of mold, mildew, algae or moss, when it is alive.... After it dies, it turns black. When surfaces begin to turn green and black, it diminishes not only the appearance, but deeply embeds into the substrate, turning it soft.
My patio is completly black, will it come clean?
In most cases yes. However, sometimes the mold can be there so long, it results in very deep staining, and may reuire stripping a thin layer of top surface off to remove it.
Can I paint, stain, or seal, over my dirty patio?
Painting, staining, or sealing is the best way to protect outdoor surfaces, it makes it .feel smoother, and makes it look better. However, putting any kind of coating over dirt, slime, mold, and mildew, will only result in the paint failing very quickly. It is important the surface is thoroughly cleaned before any coating is applied.
A lot of spots are splitting and cracking, is there anything I can do to save it?
Yes! If your patio, driveway,walkways, etc. is getting to the point of a lot of splitting and cracking, there are several options you have to very affordably fix and save what you have.

Some things you cam do fill the cracks to stop moisture and further cracking. As for the rest of it, one optin is to apply a very thick paint, this will help fill the splits and cracks, which not only makes it look better, but makes the surface much smoother and protects from further damage.
Do you offer coating options?
Yes! We we offer many coating options for you're patio and surfaces. You can choose from, paint, filler paint, clear sealer, transparent stain, semi transparent stain, solid stain, and more. The condition of the surface, and the look you want will determine what type of coating is best.
